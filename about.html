A fair shot at the table

Why this exists, how the data works, and how we handle your information.

Why MyClinical Growth exists

NHS procurement opportunities and UK digital health funding are scattered across dozens of portals, frameworks and funders. The information is public and free. It just isn't surfaced anywhere a founder would reliably see it. A £750m framework can land with near-invisible communications, and the companies that should be bidding find out too late.

MyClinical Growth is the surfacing layer. It aggregates every source, adds a plain-English read on what each opportunity actually means, and delivers it as a daily brief. It is free to browse, open by design, and built so UK healthtech gets a fair shot at the table. It is part of the MyClinical line.

How the data works

  • Find a Tender and Contracts Finder are pulled automatically via their open data APIs, filtered to NHS and health-sector buyers with a digital-health signal.
  • Frameworks, grants and the wider directory are tracked and maintained editorially. Many of these sources have no machine-readable feed.
  • The "what it means" notes are editorial judgement, not automated text. That is the part that turns a notice into a decision.
  • Everything links back to the original source. We aggregate and signal; we don't replace the official portals.

Privacy policy

Last updated: 17 May 2026. This policy is written in plain English. For absolute legal certainty, have it reviewed by a UK GDPR-savvy lawyer before you build a large list.

Who we are

MyClinical Growth is a free aggregation resource for UK NHS procurement and digital health funding. It is part of the MyClinical line, operated from the United Kingdom. The data controller is Luke Kellaway, contactable at growth@myclinical.co.uk.

What we collect, and when

Browsing the site collects no personal data. We do not set tracking cookies, we do not use third-party advertising, and we do not run any analytics that identify individual visitors.

If you subscribe to the daily brief, we collect:

  • Your email address
  • The timestamp of your subscription and any subsequent open or click events tracked by our email provider

If you submit one of the "register your interest" forms on the Bid Writers or Capital pages, we collect:

  • Your name, email address, company or firm name, and website
  • Any additional details you choose to share in the message field

Why we collect it (lawful basis)

For the daily brief: your consent, given when you tick the subscribe form. You can withdraw it at any time using the one-click unsubscribe in every email.

For the interest forms: legitimate interest in evaluating and contacting prospective directory partners. You can ask us to delete your submission at any point.

Where your data lives

Email subscribers are stored in Mailchimp (Intuit Inc., US-based, with UK and EU data processing options enabled where available). Mailchimp's privacy policy: intuit.com/privacy/statement.

Interest form submissions are stored in Netlify Forms (Netlify Inc., US-based) and forwarded by email to our MyClinical inbox. Netlify's privacy policy: netlify.com/privacy.

We rely on the standard contractual clauses provided by both vendors as the transfer mechanism for any data leaving the UK or EEA.

How long we keep it

Email subscriptions are kept for as long as you remain subscribed. If you unsubscribe, we remove your address from active sending lists and retain only the minimum record needed to prove we honoured your unsubscribe request (this is required by UK GDPR Article 21).

Interest form submissions are kept for up to 24 months from the date of submission, then deleted, unless we are in active conversation with you.

What we never do

  • We never sell, rent or share your email address with third parties.
  • We never use your email for retargeting, advertising profiles, or any non-MyClinical-Growth purpose.
  • We never enrich your data with third-party sources (no LinkedIn lookup, no firmographic enrichment).
  • We never use AI to process your personal data in a way that makes decisions about you.

Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, port or object to the processing of your personal data. To exercise any of these rights, email growth@myclinical.co.uk and we will respond within 30 days.

If you believe we have mishandled your data, you can complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint. We would rather you came to us first so we can fix it.

Changes to this policy

We will update this page when our practices change. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent change. If a change materially affects what we do with subscriber data, we will email subscribers before it takes effect.

Get in touch

Questions, corrections, a source we have missed, or a feature request: email growth@myclinical.co.uk and we will sort it.
